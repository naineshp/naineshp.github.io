Chaos and the pendulum

    As part of my degree I carried out an investigation into chaos in a pendulum. Within this project the simple pendulum, the double pendulum and the inverted pendulum was investigated, with a different approach required for each. Initially we look at simple linear models with analytical solutions but quickly move onto developing nonlinear models which are solved using computer programs such as Maple and Matlab. The ODE’s derived consider resistive parameters, and general amplitude approximations. A simple pendulum model is adapted to consider the double pendulum and investigate, in detail, how the initial conditions have a significant impact on the future motion of the pendulum giving us the evidence of chaotic behaviour. Finally, we go on to consider the inverted pendulum, deriving governing equations for the motion that can be written in the form of Mathieu’s equations and subsequently look into the stability regions. An interesting phenomenon is where a pendulum that hangs vertically downwards can be inverted by simply oscillating its pivot point at a specific drive frequency and drive amplitude. This work extends an investigation into solutions for the case of an inverted pendulum obtained by Acheson, D.J. 1999 From Calculus to Chaos.
