Pen plotter

    The design for the pen plotter which was made is essentially done by making an 2D XY platform.

    The XY platform was made using aluminium extrusions and the linear acctuator motion comprises of a belt and pinion system that is driven by stepper motors, below is a drawing of platform that was assembled.

    The central plate is given below, the 2 larger holes are where the motors are mounted on opposite sides, and the rest are for wheels so that the aluminium extrusion can run freely in both directions.

    An Arduino was used for interpreting movement and the motors were driven using the DRV8825 chip. Details of the pinouts are given below

    GRBL, a well documented motion control software for CNC machines was installed on the Arduino. GRBL takes G-code (a standardised code format for CNC machines) and outputs logical motor direction and step pulses to the motor driver. At the higher level a number of Matlab scripts were written starting by taking an image, detecting an outline, determining a tool path and the required G-code. The quality and speed of the image produced is highly dependent of the efficiency of the scripts that are written, at present the Matlab scripts are a work in progress.
